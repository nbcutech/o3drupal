        <div class='section'><p>A moist cake that keeps well.</p></div>
      
      
        <h4 class='section-title'>Yield</h4>
        <div class='section'><p>makes 8 servings<br /></p></div>
      

      <h4 class='section-title'>Ingredients</h4>
      <div class="section">
        
          <ul class="ingredient-group">
            
<li>1&frac34; cups all-purpose <span>flour</span></li>
<li>1&frac12; tsp <span>baking powder</span></li>
<li>6 tbsp <span>butter</span>, plus more for the pan</li>
<li>2/3 cup packed <span>light brown sugar</span></li>
<li>3 ripe <span>bananas</span></li>
<li>&frac12; cup plain low-fat <span>yogurt</span></li>
<li>2 large <span>eggs</span></li>
<li>&frac34; cup <span>walnuts</span>, chopped</li>
          </ul>
        
      </div>

      <h4 class='section-title'>Directions</h4>
      <div class="section"><p>Prepare ahead</p>
<p>Will keep for up to 1 week in an airtight container.</p>
<p>1. Preheat the oven to 350&deg;F (180&deg;C). Butter the loaf pan and line the bottom with wax paper. Sift the flour and baking powder together into a bowl. Add the butter and use your fingertips to rub it in until the mixture resembles bread crumbs. Stir in the sugar.</p>
<p>2. Mash the bananas with a fork'you should have 1 cup. Add the bananas, yogurt, and eggs to the flour mixture and stir until combined. Stir in the walnuts. Spread in the pan.</p>
<p>3. Bake for 1&frac14; hours, or until a wooden toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Transfer to a wire cake rack and let cool 5 minutes. Invert onto the rack, remove the paper, and cool completely.</p>
<p></p></div>

        <div class="section"><p>Special Equipment:8 &times; 4in (20 &times; 10cm) loaf pan</p></div>
